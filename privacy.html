Privacy Policy

Last updated: September 2025

At Gbero, we respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This policy explains how we collect, use, store and protect your information when you use our website and services. It also outlines your rights under the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023. By accessing or using Gbero, you consent to the practices described here.

1. What data we collect

When you register or use our platform, we may collect the following information:

  • Identity & contact details: your full name, phone number, and email address.
  • Driver information (drivers only): car model, licence plate, driver’s licence number, uploaded licence document and National Identification Number (NIN).
  • Wallet & transaction data: wallet balances, deposits, ride payments and transfers between passengers and drivers.
  • Trip details: origin and destination locations, departure time, trip type (one‑way or return) and seat availability.
  • Communication data: chat messages exchanged between passengers and drivers on our platform.
  • Usage data: information about how you use our website, such as pages visited and features used, collected via cookies.

2. How we use your data

We process your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Providing our services: to register you as a user, verify your identity, schedule and manage rides, process payments and facilitate real‑time chat between passengers and drivers.
  • Compliance and safety: to verify drivers’ credentials (licence and NIN) and ensure rides are safe and lawful.
  • Communication: to notify drivers of ride requests, send confirmation messages, respond to inquiries and provide customer support.
  • Improving the platform: to understand how our services are used and to develop new features or improve functionality.
  • Legal obligations: to comply with applicable laws, regulations and NDPA requirements.

3. Legal basis for processing

We rely on several lawful bases as defined in the NDPA to process your data:

  • Consent: by registering and providing your details, you consent to us processing your personal data for the purposes described. You may withdraw your consent at any time.
  • Contractual necessity: processing is necessary to perform the contract between you and Gbero (e.g., facilitating ride bookings and payments).
  • Legal obligation: in some cases we may need to process data to comply with a legal requirement.
  • Legitimate interests: we may use your data to improve our services and ensure security, provided that these interests do not override your rights.

4. Retention of your data

We will keep your personal data only as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including any legal, accounting or reporting requirements. Registration records and trip data may be retained for up to two years after your last activity to comply with regulatory obligations and resolve disputes. After that, data will be securely deleted or anonymised.

5. How we store and protect your data

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to safeguard your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, destruction or alteration. Registration and trip information is temporarily stored in our database (or browser storage in this prototype) and is only accessible to authorised personnel. Uploaded documents may be stored securely for verification purposes. We do not sell your data to third parties, and we only share your information when necessary to provide our services or if required by law.

6. Your rights

Under the NDPA, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Access: to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: to request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Deletion: to request that we delete your data when it is no longer needed or if you withdraw consent.
  • Restriction: to request that we limit the processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Objection: to object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
  • Portability: to request transfer of your data to you or another service provider.
  • Withdrawal of consent: to withdraw your consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before your withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below.

7. Cookies and tracking technologies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us remember your preferences, keep you logged in and improve site functionality. We only use necessary and functional cookies; we do not use cookies for advertising purposes. When you first visit Gbero, a cookie banner will ask for your consent to place cookies on your device. You may withdraw your consent at any time by clearing your browser’s local storage or cookies.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in the law, our services or technology. We will post the updated version on this page and indicate the revision date above. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

9. Contact us

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or wish to exercise your data protection rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer at privacy@gbero.com.

Gbero takes data protection seriously and is committed to complying with the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 and other applicable laws.
